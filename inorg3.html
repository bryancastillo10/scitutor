Intermolecular Forces of Attraction

In terms of the interaction of molecules to another molecules, there are forces of attraction or repulsion involved on them that can exhbit their strength or stability. These forces are called intermolecular forces of attraction or "imfa" in initials. They arise from electrostatic nature wherein it shows how positively charged ions and negatively charged ions interact with each other. These forces are the main reason on how molecules are arranged in different states of matter such as solid, liquid, and gases. Solids have compact molecules, liquid has molecules with a little bit farther molecules and its properties can be related to intermolecular forces of attraction. For gases, intermolecular forces of attraction are demonstrated on a non-ideal behavior of gases. Enumerating each of these forces, they are ion-ion interaction, dipole-dipole interaction, hydrogen bonding, and London dispersion forces.

A demonstration of dipole-dipole interaction between CO molecules, C bears the partially positive charge while O has the partially negative charge. With these charges, always remember that opposite attracts while same charges repel each other.
  • Ion-ion interaction − the strongest among the intermolecular attractive forces by which it consists oppositely charged molecules. It is observed on compounds in ionic bonding. The strength of this interaction depends on the charge and size of the ion.

  • Dipole-dipole interaction − the interaction between polar molecules. The larger the dipole moment, the larger the force as clearly explained by Coulombâ€™s law. Molecules with permanent dipole moment tend to align with opposite polarities.

  • Hydrogen Bonding − a special type of dipole-dipole interaction by which hydrogen atoms are involved. It is an interaction between a hydrogen atom and an electronegative atom. The three most common elements that participate in hydrogen bonding are nitrogen (N-H), oxygen (O-H), and fluorine (F-H). Interaction between water molecules (H2O) is an example of hydrogen bonding.

  • London Dispersion Forces − the force that is present in all molecules. It is also the only force exist between nonpolar molecules. This force can also be called induced dipole-induced dipole interaction. London dispersion forces strength is dependent on the polarizability of the electron cloud (higher LDF strength, higher molecular weight but branching of molecules should be decreasing)

Liquid State Properties

The liquid state is known to have a definite volume but its shape is not wherein it takes the shape of its container. With regards to the movements of the molecules at liquid state, it is the intermediate between solids and gases. The molecules are far enough from each other that they have spaces and their position is not fixed. With that general arrangement of molecules at liquid state, there are several properties that are observed in them which can be correlated to the intermolecular forces of attraction.

Solid State Chemistry

Another state of matter to be discussed in this section are solids. Solids are mainly characterized by having its definite volume and shape that makes it rigid. At the molecular level, its molecules are tightly packed to each other which resists sudden changes in shape or volume. With this property, solids have geometric shapes that could either be in crystal lattice or irregularly shaped (amorphous solids). To visualize the shapes of solid molecules, it can be fundamentally divided into cubic unit cells that represents their crystal lattice.

As you can see on the table of cubic unit cells, there are three basic types of it namely simple cubic cell, body-centered cell, and face-centered cell. There are still more types of crystal lattice at solid state which are commonly studied thoroughly in the field of material sciences and engineering. Some of those other crystal lattice include orthorhombic, rhombohedral, monoclinic, triclinic, and hexagonal which also exhibits different structural properties. For simple cubic cell, each of the atoms involved is placed on the corners of a cube which signifies that there is 1 atom per cell (8 atoms x 1/8). At body-centered cell, there are still atoms placed on the corners of a cube but this time there is an additional atom which is placed at the center of the cube. It shows that there is (8 atoms x 1/8) + 1 atom = 2 atoms per unit cell. For the face-centered cell, the atoms are placed in the corners and faces of the cube which shows (6 x 1/2 + 8 x 1/8) = 4 atoms per unit cell. These set of classifications are based on the atoma arrangement in a crystal lattice.

Another set of classifications for solid crystals depends on the forces that holds each of the unit cells together and also their chemical properties they exhibit. These classifications include ionic, molecular, covalent (network), and metallic solids. In ionic crystals, the structure can be made up of ions of a variety of ions with different sizes and charges which are held together by electrostatic forces. Many ionic compounds can be in the orientation of a simple cubic or face-centered cubic structure. Molecular crystals have their units holded by IMFA and one common example of it is solid water (ice). Covalent crystals are composed of lattice points that is occupied by atoms. It is commonly observed on different forms of carbon complexes such as diamonds and graphite. Metallic solids are emphasizes the metallic properties such as good conductors of heat and electricty, malleability, and ductility. This classification of crystal is manifested by all metals at solid state.

Band Theory of Metals

Focusing on the properties of metallic elements, a theory was derived based on the delocalization of the valence electrons resulting from them to be a good counductor of electricity. This theory is the band theory of metals which also emphasizes how conductors, semiconductors, and insulators works. A conductor is a material which is best for allowing heat and electricty to flow freely. On the other hand, insulators are non-conducting materials which has few mobile electrons that results for heat and electrical conductance suppresion.

In band theory, it shows the movement of electrons as it jumps to a higher energy level whenever there is conduction of heat or electricity occurs. The regions where these electrons interact are called valence bands and conduction bands. This jumping of electrons to a higher energy level follows the quantum theories on particle-wave duality. Valence bands contains the electrons at the highestenergy level while next to it is the conduction band and it is relatively an empty band. In these bands, there is a gap or open space which is called band gap that is the distance separating them. Band gaps differ depending if the substance is a conductor, semiconductor, or insulator. A conductor has overlapping band gaps which results to let the electrons move freely. Insulators has a very large band gap by which electrons are not capable enough to jump to a higher energy level. Semiconductors have narrow gaps by which some electrons can jump over to a higher energy level. As stated, semiconductors are the intermediate between conductors and insulators. Semiconductors can be added with some impurities to either enhance or decrease its conductivity. The process of adding it is called doping.

For semiconductors,

  1. p-type semiconductor

    2. This semiconductor is an electron-poor type. The added material has fewer valence electrons than the host material

Nomenclature of Metal-Ligand Complexes

It is common that inorganic compounds are metal-ligand complexes or organometallic compounds. These are compounds generally composed of a central metal atom and substituents called ligands. The chemical bonding with the metal and ligands could either be covalent or ionic. In this section, we will determine the appropriate naming of this type of compounds with the following set of rules.

  • Rule 1: For neutral coordination complexes, their names are without spaces. For Ionic coordination complexes, cation is named first followed by the anion with a space between them.
