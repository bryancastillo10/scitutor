Interesting Trivia and Facts

Number of human bones is greater on babies than the adults

The bones in the human skeleton fuses together as the human grows. Initially, a baby has about 270 bones by which most of it are cartilages while adults decreases its number of bones up to 206 bones. The smallest bone is the stapes found in the middle air while femur (thighbone) is the largest among the bones.

Planet Earth is a giant magnet

In studying Earth science, the inner core (part of the layer of the Earth) is composed of solid iron surrounded by molten liquid iron. At this state, temperature and density varies resulting to create currents in the iron. As the Earth rotates, those currents combine with each other to form a large magnetic field. Today, we apply this principle with the use of compasses in locating places. Its needle responses to the magnetic field involved in the core.

The human stomach acid is strong enough to dissolve a stainless steel

As we digests food, our stomach produces a highly corrosive acid (known as peptic acid in biochemistry or hydrochloric acid in chemistry) with a pH scale from 2 to 3. This acid can also attack your stomach linings which is the cause of hyperacidity disorder. Through the buffering effect of bicarbonate solution produced, your stomach linings were protected from the corrosive acid.
